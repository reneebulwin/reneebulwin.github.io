You Are Not Alone

For the ones still carrying stories they were never supposed to carry alone — this space is for you. A collection of gentle reminders, reflection prompts, and grounding practices to support you wherever you are on the journey.

"You are not alone. And there is something powerful waiting on the other side of breathing."

Reflection Prompts

Questions to Sit With

  • "When did I first learn to be quiet about what hurt me — and what would it feel like to stop?"
  • "Some truths don't disappear — they wait. What truth have I been gently waiting to hear from myself?"
  • "Where in my body do I feel safe today? If I don't feel safe, what is one small comfort I can offer myself right now?"
  • "How did I show resilience this week, even in ways that felt invisible to others?"
  • "What does it mean to become who I was meant to be — beyond what survival required of me?"
  • "What was buried that is beginning, slowly, to surface — and can I meet it with compassion instead of fear?"
Wellness Notes

Things Worth Remembering

Silence Was Never Where Healing Was Meant to Live

For many of us, silence became safety. We learned early that speaking up cost too much. But healing asks something different of us — not loudness, but honesty. Even a whisper of truth, spoken to yourself, is a beginning.

You Were Not Strong — You Were Unsupported

There is a difference between strength and the armor we build when no one shows up. What felt like resilience was often survival. And now, healing means we get to put some of that armor down — gently, in safe spaces, when we're ready.

What Once Felt Like Destruction

The moments that felt like breaking points — they were also turning points. Healing often looks like chaos before it looks like clarity. The unraveling is not the end of the story. It is the beginning of the finding.

Healing Begins When You Finally Feel Seen

You don't have to perform wellness. You don't have to arrive somewhere before you deserve care. Exactly where you are — with exactly what you are carrying — is enough to begin.

Grounding Practices

Ways to Come Back to Yourself

5-4-3-2-1 Sensory Grounding

When feeling overwhelmed, pause. Name 5 things you can see, 4 you can feel, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, and 1 you can taste. This gently brings the mind back to the present moment.

The Hand-on-Heart Breath

Place one hand on your heart and one on your belly. Breathe in slowly for a count of four, hold for two, and exhale for six. Focus on the warmth of your hands — the body remembering that it is here, and it is held.

Rooting the Feet

Sit or stand with feet flat on the floor. Press your toes and heels gently into the ground. Imagine roots extending from your feet deep into the earth — holding you steady, holding you here.
