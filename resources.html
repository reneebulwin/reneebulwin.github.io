Resources & Reflection

For the ones still carrying stories they were never supposed to carry alone.

A collection of gentle reminders, reflection prompts, and grounding practices to support you wherever you are in the process.

Please note: This content is for educational and wellness support purposes only. It is not a replacement for therapy, medical care, or crisis support. If you are in crisis, please contact the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline by calling or texting 988.

Reflection Prompts

  • "What did I first learn to be quiet about — and what would it look like to say it out loud?"
  • "Some truths don't disappear — they wait. What have I been quietly waiting to let myself feel?"
  • "Where do I feel it is safe to fall apart — and what does that say about where I feel truly visible?"
  • "How did I learn who I was allowed to be — and how much of that still feels like a choice right now?"
  • "How did I feel the first time someone truly listened — and what did that open up in me?"
  • "What has brought me to a healing space — and am I approaching it with compassion instead of fear?"

Wellness Notes

Silence Isn't Always Healing

At a certain point, silence became safety — not honesty. Real healing asks us, eventually, to find our witness.

You Were Not Strong — You Were Surviving

There is a difference between strength and survival. What you often mistook for strength was you carrying what adults should have carried — and that was never fair to you.

What Looks Like Shutting Down

Sometimes the mind creates distance to protect you. It is the body's way of trying to keep you alive. It is not weakness.

You Don't Have to Arrive Before You Begin

You don't have to be healed before you start healing. You are exactly where you need to be to take the next small step.

Grounding Practices

5-4-3-2-1 Sensory Grounding

Name 5 things you can see, 4 things you can hear, 3 things you can smell, 2 things you can touch, and 1 thing you can taste. It brings the mind back to the present moment.

The Hand-on-Heart Breath

Place one hand on your heart and breathe slowly. Inhale for a count of four, hold for four, exhale for four. The rhythm of your own heartbeat brings the body back to itself.

Rooting the Body

Sit with both feet flat on the floor. Press them gently into the ground. Imagine roots extending downward. You are grounded. You are here. You are safe right where you are.
